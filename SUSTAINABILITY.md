# Sustainability Model for BOSC Community Library

## Overview
As the Lead Community Maintainer, I propose a hybrid sustainability model to ensure the BOSC Community Library remains a viable, high-quality resource for the Bugema community and beyond.

## Funding Strategy: Service-Based vs. Foundation-Driven

### 1. The Service-Based Model (The "Red Hat" Approach)
This model focuses on providing value-added services around the open-source core. 
- **Implementation:** BOSC will offer premium technical support, custom implementation for schools, and training workshops for educators.
- **Benefits:** Generates direct revenue and builds professional capacity within the community.
- **Trade-offs:** Requires a dedicated team and may prioritize corporate/institutional needs over individual contributors.

### 2. The Foundation-Driven Model
This model relies on a non-profit entity to steward the project.
- **Implementation:** Establishing a BOSC Foundation to seek grants from organizations like UNESCO or the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, and corporate sponsorships from tech giants.
- **Benefits:** Ensures the project remains focused on its mission of public-sector transparency and educational equity.
- **Trade-offs:** Funding can be cyclical and dependent on external priorities.

## Final Recommendation: The Hybrid Strategy
We will establish a BOSC Foundation to manage the core repository and standards, while fostering a local ecosystem of service providers who can bid for government and institutional contracts.
